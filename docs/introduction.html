Species Manual for Modeling Lichens, Mosses, Vascular Plants, and Soil Mites

2 Introduction

This document describes the content, methods, and limitations/caveats associated with the species models developed by the ABMI. Species models are currently accessible in two locations:

  • Data & Analytics Portal (http://www.abmi.ca/home/data-analytics; launched December 2016): The ABMI Data & Analytics (DA) Portal was developed, and is maintained by the ABMI Information Centre. This portal serves to present a variety of ABMI data and mapping products and allows users to access and interact with ABMI data in a variety of ways.

  • Species Development Website (https://science.abmi.ca/): The ABMI Species Development Website was developed, and is maintained by the ABMI Science Centre. This site allows for the Science Centre to review our species models before making them available on the Data & Analytics Portal. We recommend that users only use the information available on the Data & Analytics Portal or through our R packages (e.g., wildRtrax, allinone).

2.1 Rationale for presenting model results

The ABMI collects data on thousands of species, and evaluates how their relative abundance varies among natural and human-created habitats. It is a core mission of the ABMI to make both the raw data and model results available for public use. However, some species are not well detected using the ABMI sampling method, others are detected a few times, while some species have habitat models which produce implausible results. Therefore this document aims to improve the transparency around how our raw species data is used to create habitat models.
